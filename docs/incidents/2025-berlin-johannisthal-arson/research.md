## Root Cause Analysis

Deliberate sabotage of power distribution infrastructure. Part of an escalating pattern of attacks on Berlin's grid.

### Contributing Factors

- Vulnerable above-ground/accessible infrastructure
- Politically motivated sabotage (suspected)
- Limited physical security on distribution-level assets

### Cascade Mechanism

Not a technical cascade — direct physical destruction of infrastructure.

## Grid Context

Berlin's distribution grid has been subject to multiple deliberate attacks. This was the first major arson incident, preceding the 2026 Teltow Canal attack.

## Response & Recovery

Power restored after repair of damaged infrastructure. Heightened security awareness but limited physical hardening of distribution assets.

## VPP Relevance

- **Response time gap:** N/A — physical destruction, not frequency event
- **Flexibility gap:** Homes with batteries and islanding capability could have maintained power during the outage
- **Architecture lesson:** Distributed energy resources provide resilience against physical attacks on centralized infrastructure. A home with solar + battery can island and maintain critical loads even when the grid is physically severed.
