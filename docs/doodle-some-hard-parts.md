# Hard Parts to Doodle Through

## Slide Ordering & Narrative Flow Issues

### "The Old Playbook" (slide 11) — Curtailment Doesn't Belong
- The Old Playbook is about the **pre-renewable toolkit**: peakers, spinning reserves, load shedding
- These are crude, expensive, fossil-era tools
- **Renewable curtailment doesn't belong here** — it's a symptom of the new problem, not an old solution
- Options:
  - Remove the curtailment box entirely
  - Replace with **"Over-provisioning"** — building 15-20% more capacity than needed "just in case" (the true old-world analog)
- Curtailment hits properly on slide 23 ("Clean Energy Has Outgrown the Grid") where it belongs — as proof that renewables broke the old model

### "Demand Response" (slide 17) — Way Too Early
- Currently sits **before** the Renewable Revolution section title
- Audience hasn't heard about duck curves or curtailment yet — no context for why DR matters
- Natural home is **Act 3, near VPP flexibility** — DR is a tool a VPP uses, not a standalone concept

### Proposed Reorder
```
ACT 1: THE GRID (problem: fragility)
  ...
  Old Playbook: peakers, reserves, load shedding (NO curtailment)
  ...
  "No flexibility" bridge

ACT 2: THE RENEWABLE REVOLUTION (problem: variability)
  Section Title
  Renewable Growth
  Duck Curve
  Curtailment ("Outgrown the Grid")  ← renewable curtailment lives HERE

ACT 3: THE VIRTUAL POWER PLANT (solution)
  ...
  Impact of Flexibility
  Consumers Become Infrastructure
  Demand Response in Action  ← moved HERE, as a VPP capability demo
  ...
```

### Key Insight
The story arc should be:
1. **Act 1**: The grid is fragile (Texas, cascades, old blunt tools)
2. **Act 2**: Renewables make it harder (growth, duck curve, curtailment)
3. **Act 3**: VPPs solve it — and demand response is one of the tools a VPP uses
