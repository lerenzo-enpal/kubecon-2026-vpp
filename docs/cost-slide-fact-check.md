# Cost of Stability Slide — Fact-Check & Double-Counting Analysis

## The Problem

The original slide added EUR 6.5B + EUR 11B + EUR 3B = EUR 20B. This double-counts EUR 6.5B because capacity mechanisms are a subset of the ACER security-of-supply figure.

## Category Breakdown

### 1. Security of Supply (ACER 2025): EUR 11B/yr
- **Source:** ACER "Security of EU Electricity Supply 2025" report
- **Covers:** ~40 national measures across the EU including:
  - **Capacity mechanisms: EUR 6.5B** (paying gas peakers to sit idle — this is a SUBSET, not additive)
  - Strategic reserves: ~EUR 2B
  - Flexibility schemes + other national measures: ~EUR 2.5B
- **Does NOT include:** ancillary services (spinning reserves), congestion management, curtailment compensation

### 2. Congestion Management (EU-wide): EUR 4.2B/yr
- **Source:** ACER Congestion Management report 2024
- **Covers:** Redispatch, countertrading, curtailment compensation across EU
- **Germany specifically:** EUR 2.78B in 2024 (EUR 3B in 2023)
  - Curtailment compensation: EUR 554M (2024)
  - Fixed + activation costs: EUR 1.03B (2024)
  - Countertrading and other: remainder
- **No overlap** with the EUR 11B security-of-supply figure

### 3. Spinning Reserves: ~15% of generation capacity
- **Source:** Argonne National Lab (standard operating reserve margin)
- **Not a direct EUR figure** — this is generation capacity held back as frequency response
- The EU ancillary services market (FCR, aFRR, mFRR) is estimated at ~EUR 30B/yr (Market Research Future 2024), but this is a market value, not pure waste
- **No overlap** with EUR 11B or EUR 4.2B — ancillary services are separately procured

### 4. Peaker Plants
- Peaker plants are **inside capacity mechanisms (EUR 6.5B)**, which is **inside security of supply (EUR 11B)**
- They are NOT a separate cost line
- The EUR 6.5B is what EU member states pay gas turbines to be available (run ~5% of the year, idle ~95%)
- If calling them out: use as a sub-detail of the EUR 11B, e.g. "including EUR 6.5B paying gas peakers to sit idle"

### 5. Curtailment (Germany)
- **2024:** 9.34 TWh curtailed, EUR 554M compensation paid
- **2023:** 10.48 TWh curtailed, EUR 577M compensation paid
- The "19 TWh" figure from 2023 is TOTAL congestion management volume (all redispatch measures), not just renewable curtailment
- Curtailment compensation is inside the EUR 2.78B German congestion management figure, which is inside the EUR 4.2B EU-wide figure

## Accurate Non-Overlapping Totals

| Category | Amount | Overlap? |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Security of Supply (incl. capacity mechanisms, peakers) | EUR 11B/yr | None |
| Congestion Management (incl. curtailment) | EUR 4.2B/yr | None |
| Spinning Reserves | 15% margin (qualitative) | Not EUR-denominated |
| **Total (additive EUR)** | **~EUR 15B/yr** | |

## Recommended Slide Structure

Three cards (no double counting):
1. **Spinning Reserves** — 15% of capacity burns fuel 24/7 just to be ready
2. **Security of Supply** — EUR 11B/yr across the EU, including EUR 6.5B paying gas peakers to sit idle
3. **Congestion & Curtailment** — EUR 4.2B/yr EU-wide; 10 TWh of clean energy wasted in Germany alone

Bottom line: "Over EUR 15 billion per year — and when it fails, Texas happens."
